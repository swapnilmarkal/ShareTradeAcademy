Frequently Asked Questions

Will I make good money after taking this course?+

This course teaches you practical trading skills, strategies, and risk management. However, trading outcomes depend on your discipline, market conditions, and practice. We focus on helping you become a consistent and informed trader rather than promising fixed income.

Do I need any prior knowledge of the stock market?+

No. The course is designed for complete beginners. We start from the basics like market structure, candlestick reading, and gradually move to advanced trading strategies.

How much capital do I need to start trading?+

You can start with any amount you are comfortable with. However, we recommend starting small while you learn and focusing more on skill development rather than high capital exposure.

Is trading risky?+

Yes. Trading involves financial risk. That's why our program strongly emphasizes risk management, capital protection, and disciplined trading strategies.

How long does it take to learn trading?+

The learning process varies from person to person. Most students begin understanding core concepts within a few weeks, but becoming consistently profitable requires practice and experience in live markets.

Will I get personal mentorship?+

Yes, mentorship is part of our advanced programs where students receive guidance, trade reviews, and feedback from experienced traders.

Can I trade part-time while working or studying?+

Yes, our program is designed to be flexible, allowing you to trade part-time while managing other commitments.

Do you guarantee profits?+

No. We do not guarantee profits. The stock market is unpredictable, and success depends on skill, discipline, and risk management. Our goal is to build your understanding and trading capability.

Will you provide live market guidance?+

Yes. We conduct live market sessions for educational purposes where we explain how markets move in real time, how to analyze charts, and how professional traders approach decision-making. These sessions are designed to help students learn observation, analysis, and trading discipline—not to provide any buy/sell recommendations or assured trade calls.
